Spanish Sentence Completion Norms

Please cite the following if you make use of these norms in your research:

McDonald, S. A. & Tamariz, M. (2002). Completion norms for 112 Spanish sentences. Behavior Research Methods, Instruments, and Computers, 34, 128-137.

Below is an example sentence frame from the dataset, the set of responses produced with their production probabilities (calculated using Schwanenfluegel's (1986) multiple production method), and four measures of contextual constraint. The four measures are computed as follows:

  • CS=constraint strength (the production probability of the most expected completion word).

  • TTR=type-token ratio (the number of unique responses divided by the total number of responses).

  • R=redundancy (an information-theoretic estimate of how much information about the sentence-final word is contained in the previous context).

  • MR=mean number of responses (the total number of responses produced divided by the number of subjects producing at least one response).

    8. En la plaza mayor la gente suele dar de comer a las ___.

    palomas (0.91) aves (0.3) ratas (0.09) hormigas (0.09) golondrinas (0.09) gaviotas (0.09) vacas (0.04) tres (0.04) necesitadas (0.04) mascotas (0.04) gallinas (0.04)
    CS=0.913; TTR=0.268; MR=1.783; R=0.297

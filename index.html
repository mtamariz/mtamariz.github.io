Monica Tamariz

Assistant Professor
Psychology
Heriot-Watt University

I study the cultural evolution of language. I am also interested in cultural-evolutionary explanations of other human communication systems, for instance the system used by Argentine tango dancers, and of other cultural domains, such as technology or art. I am an Associate member of the Centre for Language Evolution at the University of Edinburgh.

Some recent publications

Here are some publications. You can see more in google scholar.

Tamariz (2017)
Experimental studies of the cultural evolution of language

Long review of recent experiments showing how cultural evolution has been used to explain the origins of structure in linguistic signals, and in the mapping between signals and meanings.

Tamariz & Kirby (2016)
The cultural evolution of language

Short review of recent experiments showing how cultural evolution has been used to explain the origins of structure in linguistic signals, and in the mapping between signals and meanings.

Kirby, Tamariz, Cornish & Smith (2015)
Compression and communication in the cultural evolution of linguistic structure

Simulations and experiments demonstrate that linguistic structure arises from a trade-off between pressures for compressibility (imposed during learning) and expressivity (imposed during communication).

Tamariz & Kirby (2015)
Culture: Copying, compression, and conventionality

This experimental study crisply demonstrates that when behavior is transmitted through the bottleneck of learning (even if this bottleneck is as tiny as remembering a drawing for a matter of seconds), then we see a cumulative increase in compressibility.

Tamariz, Ellison, Barr & Fay (2014)
Cultural selection drives the evolution of human communication systems

The spread of graphical signal variants in a closed-group population (Fay et al. 2010) is best explained by a model that includes two types of cultural selection: content-based bias and egocentric bias.
